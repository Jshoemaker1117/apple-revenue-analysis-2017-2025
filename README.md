# Apple Product Revenue Analysis (2017–2025)

This project analyzes Apple’s product-level revenue composition using official SEC Form 10-K filings from Fiscal Year 2017 through Fiscal Year 2025.

The objective is to examine Apple’s evolving revenue structure, with particular focus on iPhone dependence, Services expansion, and long-term compounding dynamics.

---

## Objective

This analysis evaluates:

- iPhone revenue concentration and volatility  
- Services expansion and recurring monetization growth  
- Revenue mix transformation over time  
- Hardware vs. Services structural divergence  
- Long-term compounding differentials (CAGR analysis)  

---

## Key Findings

- iPhone revenue experienced sharp acceleration in Fiscal Year 2021, followed by normalization in subsequent years.
- Services revenue compounded at approximately **16% annually**, nearly **3x faster** than Hardware revenue (~6% CAGR).
- Services contribution increased from **~14% of total revenue in 2017** to **over 26% by 2025**.
- Total revenue growth (~7.7% CAGR) exceeded Hardware growth, indicating measurable lift from Services expansion.
- Apple’s revenue structure is gradually transitioning from hardware concentration toward ecosystem-driven monetization.

---

## Strategic Insight

While hardware remains the primary driver of scale, Services growth has materially reshaped Apple’s financial profile.  

The data suggests Apple is evolving from a predominantly hardware-cycle-driven company into an integrated ecosystem platform characterized by:

- Hardware as a user acquisition engine  
- Services as a recurring monetization engine  
- Increasing revenue stability over time  
- Reduced concentration risk tied to product supercycles  

This transformation enhances long-term resilience and structural durability.
